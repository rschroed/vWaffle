# vvaffle Product Vision

## Product Statement / Point of View

`vvaffle` is built on a simple belief: recognition works best when it is easy to give, visible to the people around us, and warm enough to feel human. Most teams do not need a complex employee-recognition platform to appreciate one another. They need a lightweight way to say, "that mattered," in a format that feels immediate, social, and worth repeating.

`vvaffle` exists to make small moments of appreciation visible. It turns gratitude into a simple team habit instead of a private aside or a formal process.

## The Problem

Today, team appreciation often falls into one of two extremes.

At one extreme, appreciation is invisible. Someone says thanks in a direct message, in a hallway, or at the end of a meeting, and the moment disappears. The recipient feels good, but the team does not see what happened, the behavior is not reinforced, and the moment does not build shared culture.

At the other extreme, recognition is heavy. Many systems are designed for formal programs, manager workflows, point economies, or company-wide ceremonies. Those tools can be useful, but they are often too slow, too structured, or too performative for everyday peer appreciation. When recognition feels like paperwork, people stop using it.

Small teams need something in the middle: lightweight enough to use in seconds, visible enough to shape culture, and playful enough that people want to come back to it.

## The Core Idea

`vvaffle` is a public, lightweight recognition product for small internal teams. A waffle is a small social unit of appreciation: one person sends it, another person receives it, and the team sees the moment in a shared feed.

The waffle mechanic matters because it gives recognition a distinct shape. It is more expressive than a bare "like" and less ceremonious than an award. It makes appreciation feel intentional without making it heavy.

The product is not trying to quantify human value. It is trying to make gratitude easy to notice and easy to repeat.

## How It Works

The core social loop is simple.

A sender notices something worth appreciating. They send a waffle to a recipient with a short message and a flavor that adds a bit of personality. That waffle appears in the shared feed for the team. The recipient gets a visible moment of appreciation, and everyone else gets a small reminder of what good work, generosity, or collaboration looks like on this team.

Over time, that loop does more than create individual moments. It creates a lightweight public record of team behavior. People see what is celebrated. They see who is contributing. They see that appreciation is normal and easy to give.

In product terms:

- A `waffle` is a lightweight public appreciation moment with a sender, recipient, flavor, message, and timestamp.
- A `sender` is the teammate initiating recognition.
- A `recipient` is the teammate being recognized.
- A `shared feed` is the visible stream of waffles for a team.
- A `small internal team` is the initial context: a group small enough that names, moments, and social visibility still feel personal.

## v1 Product Definition

The first version of `vvaffle` is intentionally narrow.

It allows a teammate to send a waffle to another teammate with a short note, choose a flavor to give the moment some personality, and publish that waffle into a shared team feed. The feed becomes the product's center of gravity: a visible stream of appreciation that is fast to scan and easy to contribute to.

v1 prioritizes:

- peer-to-peer waffle sending
- a shared public team feed
- short-form recognition messages
- lightweight personality through waffle flavors
- minimal friction from open to send

v1 does not need to solve every team-recognition problem. It only needs to make public appreciation feel natural, fast, and rewarding enough to repeat.

## Principles / Constraints

`vvaffle` should follow a small set of product principles.

First, it must stay lightweight. Sending recognition should take seconds, not minutes.

Second, it must stay socially visible. The product works because appreciation is shared, not buried in private channels.

Third, it must feel emotionally warm. The tone should be playful and human without becoming ironic or distracting.

Fourth, it must resist unnecessary complexity. The narrower the product stays, the clearer the behavior loop becomes.

Finally, it should optimize for habit formation over feature breadth. A small product that gets used often is more valuable than a broad product that feels like work.

## What Success Looks Like

Success for `vvaffle` is not defined by novelty. It is defined by behavior.

The product is working when teammates send waffles regularly without being prompted by management, when appreciation comes from across the team rather than a single enthusiastic user, and when the shared feed feels like a living reflection of team culture rather than an empty timeline.

In practical terms, success looks like:

- repeat waffle sending over time
- participation distributed across a team
- recipients feeling meaningfully recognized
- the feed becoming a lightweight cultural artifact for the group
- recognition feeling easy and genuine rather than forced

## What This Is Not

`vvaffle` is not a full workplace social network.

It is not a manager-driven performance system. It is not an HR platform. It is not a rewards marketplace, points engine, or incentive economy. It is not trying to formalize recognition into something bureaucratic.

The product should stay focused on one thing: making peer appreciation public, lightweight, and easy to repeat.

## Brief Future

If `vvaffle` works, the next layer is not to make it bigger for its own sake. It is to deepen the habit carefully.

That could include lightweight prompts that help teams remember to recognize each other, simple rituals that encourage regular participation, or persistence and identity features that make the feed feel more durable. It could also include subtle ways to help teams revisit meaningful moments over time.

The future of `vvaffle` should remain consistent with the core idea: keep appreciation visible, keep it lightweight, and keep the product small enough to feel personal.
